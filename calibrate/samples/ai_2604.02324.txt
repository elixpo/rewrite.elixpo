# AI-generated equivalent of: Grounded Token Initialization for New Vocabulary in LMs for Generative Recommendation
# Original arXiv: 2604.02324

We conduct a comprehensive empirical investigation to validate whether principled initialization of novel vocabulary entries mitigates representation degradation during domain adaptation. Our experimental framework compares three distinct initialization paradigms: (1) baseline random initialization sampling from the pretrained embedding matrix variance; (2) heuristic aggregation initializing new tokens via mean pooling of item metadata token embeddings; and (3) our proposed Grounded Initialization, which computes distributional alignment through contrastive anchoring against high-frequency semantic anchors in the pretrained vocabulary. All experiments employ a 7-billion parameter decoder-only architecture expanded to accommodate 8,192 additional SID tokens representing the item corpus.

The training protocol proceeds in two stages. First, we freeze the pretrained parameters and perform warm-up updates solely on the new token embeddings for 1,000 iterations using a contrastive objective that minimizes the Wasserstein distance between the new token distribution and a stratified sample of semantically analogous pretrained tokens. Subsequently, we conduct full-model supervised fine-tuning with standard next-token prediction loss, where the model generates a sequence of SID tokens corresponding to ground-truth relevant items. Evaluation metrics include Recall@1, Recall@10, Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR), and a novel Dispersion Index measuring the average cosine similarity between distinct item representations to quantify semantic collapse quantitatively.

On the industrial candidate retrieval dataset, Grounded Initialization demonstrates substantial improvements over the SFT baseline, achieving 23.4% relative gain in Recall@10 and 18.7% in MRR. Crucially, the Dispersion Index indicates 34% higher embedding variance compared to random initialization, suggesting effective mitigation of representation collapse. The heuristic metadata-averaging approach exhibits intermediate performance (11.2% Recall@10 improvement) but suffers from high variance across item categories with sparse textual descriptions. These gains persist across varying relevance thresholds, with particularly pronounced improvements for the marginal "partial match" category where semantic precision proves most critical.

The Vibrent Clothes Rental experiments corroborate these findings while enabling absolute metric reporting. Grounded Initialization achieves Recall@1 of 0.417 versus 0.298 for random initialization, alongside a 15.3% reduction in perplexity for generated SID sequences. Ablations varying vocabulary size from 1,024 to 16,384 tokens reveal that performance degradation in baseline methods scales linearly with vocabulary expansion (Pearson ρ = −0.89), whereas Grounded Initialization maintains stable performance within a 3% margin across all tested cardinalities. This suggests robustness to corpus scale, a critical requirement for industrial deployment where catalog sizes frequently exceed tens of thousands.

Analysis of the embedding geometry reveals that Grounded Initialization induces a structured manifold where SID vectors occupy regions of the latent space previously underutilized by natural language tokens, rather than collapsing into high-density linguistic clusters. Visualization via t-SNE projection confirms that item categories (e.g., professional attire, casual wear) form distinct, well-separated clusters with inter-cluster distances comparable to those observed between natural language semantic categories in the pretrained model. This geometric preservation explains the improved ranking metrics: items maintain discriminative boundaries rather than converging to indistinguishable representations.

However, the methodology exhibits sensitivity to anchor selection quality. When contrastive anchors are sampled uniformly from the pretrained vocabulary rather than stratified by semantic frequency, performance degrades to within 5% of the heuristic baseline, indicating that distributional alignment requires careful curation of reference points. Additionally, computational overhead increases by approximately 12% during the warm-up phase due to the Wasserstein distance computation, though this amortizes across the subsequent fine-tuning stage.

These results establish that initialization strategies significantly impact model capability in generative retrieval contexts, with geometric alignment serving as a crucial determinant of downstream task performance. The consistent gains across both proprietary recruitment data and public fashion benchmarks suggest generalizability to diverse recommendation domains characterized by large-scale vocabulary expansion. Future investigations should explore adaptive anchor selection mechanisms and extend these principles to multimodal generative retrieval where visual and textual embeddings require joint initialization.
