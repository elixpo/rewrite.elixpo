# AI-generated equivalent of: go-$m$HC: Direct Parameterization of Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections via Generalized Orthostochastic Matrices
# Original arXiv: 2604.02309

We propose a direct parameterization of the Birkhoff polytope through generalized orthostochastic matrices constructed via the matrix exponential of skew-symmetric generators. Given a learnable skew-symmetric matrix \(A \in \mathfrak{so}(d)\), we form the orthogonal matrix \(Q = \exp(A)\) through the standard matrix exponential, which maps the Lie algebra to the Lie group \(\text{SO}(d)\) while preserving differentiability. To expand beyond permutation matrices and enable dense sampling of the doubly stochastic manifold, we introduce a continuous interpolation parameter \(s \in [0,1]\) that modulates convex combinations between the uniform center \(\frac{1}{d}\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^T\) and the orthostochastic boundary \(Q \circ Q\) (where \(\circ\) denotes the Hadamard product). The resulting matrix \(M(s, A) = (1-s)\frac{1}{d}\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^T + s(Q \circ Q)\) resides strictly within the Birkhoff polytope for all valid inputs, providing exact parameterization without post-hoc projection or iterative normalization.

This construction admits efficient computation in \(O(d^3)\) time using stabilized matrix exponential algorithms, avoiding the factorial complexity inherent to Birkhoff-von Neumann decompositions. Within the Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections framework, we instantiate this as go-mHC, where the mixing weights governing information flow between residual streams are directly parameterized by \(M(s, A)\). The hyperparameter \(s\) explicitly controls the expressivity-complexity trade-off: as \(s \to 0\), the system approaches uniform averaging (computationally trivial but geometrically restrictive), while \(s \to 1\) recovers the full orthostochastic manifold which densely approximates the polytope boundary.

To characterize the volumetric coverage of our parameterization, we performed spectral analysis on ensembles generated by sampling uniformly from the skew-symmetric generator space. Eigenvalue distributions of go-mHC matrices closely approximate the theoretical Marchenko-Pastur distributions characteristic of the full Birkhoff polytope, with total variation distances significantly smaller than Kronecker-factorized baselines (\(p < 0.001\)). This indicates that go-mHC fills the doubly stochastic manifold more completely than factorized approaches, which tend to concentrate mass on low-rank submanifolds.

On synthetic stream-mixing benchmarks designed to probe the fundamental representational limits of hyper-connection mechanisms, go-mHC achieved the information-theoretic minimum loss for cross-entropy mixing tasks, whereas Kronecker methods converged to suboptimal plateaus due to their restricted rank structure. Optimization trajectories revealed that go-mHC converges up to ten times faster than exact permutation-based parameterizations, attributable to the smooth Riemannian geometry of the orthostochastic manifold which facilitates stable gradient propagation without the discontinuities introduced by sorting operations or projection steps.

Validation on a 30M parameter GPT-style language model demonstrates practical efficacy at scale. Replacing standard residual connections with go-mHC layers yielded perplexity reductions of 8.3% on WikiText-103 compared to Kronecker-factorized hyper-connections, while maintaining computational parity at approximately \(1.2 \times 10^{18}\) FLOPs. Ablations indicate that intermediate values of \(s\) (typically 0.6–0.8) provide optimal trade-offs between expressivity and training stability, suggesting that partial interpolation toward the polytope boundary suffices for natural language modeling tasks.

These results resolve the longstanding tension between exactness and efficiency in doubly stochastic parameterization. By leveraging the Lie algebraic structure of skew-symmetric matrices, we circumvent the combinatorial explosion of permutation-based representations while preserving geometric fidelity essential for learned dynamic connectivity. The efficacy of intermediate \(s\) values implies that the full factorial complexity of the Birkhoff polytope may be unnecessary for deep learning applications, with the orthostochastic boundary providing sufficient expressivity. Furthermore, the natural compositionality of this approach with Kronecker factorizations suggests a hybrid paradigm wherein go-mHC handles high-entanglement residual interactions while factorized methods manage low-rank approximations, pointing toward scalable architectures for future foundation models. Future investigations will explore adaptive annealing schedules for \(s\) during training and extensions to non-square asymmetric connectivity patterns.
